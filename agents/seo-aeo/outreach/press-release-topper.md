## Consent template

Before naming any student, get a signed consent form covering:
- Permission to use full name, age, city
- Permission to use NATA score
- Permission to publish photo
- Permission to quote the student in press materials
- Right to withdraw consent at any time (with 30-day notice)

Store consent forms in a secure folder. Never publish a student's name
without consent on file.

## Localisation

The Tamil regional press version of the release should:
- Use Tamil headline ("நீரம் கிளாஸஸ் மாணவர்கள் NATA 2026 இல் சிறப்பாக தேர்ச்சி")
- Translate the founder quote to Tamil
- Highlight Tamil Nadu-specific results in the lead paragraph
- Use Tamil currency and date format

A Tamil journalist or in-house translator should do the final pass.
Avoid machine-translated Tamil for press materials.

## Common mistakes to avoid

1. Sending the release before NATA results are officially declared
2. Publishing student names without parental consent
3. Inflating numbers (do not round 145 students to "nearly 200")
4. Mixing NATA scores with JEE scores in the same statement
5. Sending the same generic release to every publication, customise by region
6. Forgetting to include the media contact's working hours and time zone
