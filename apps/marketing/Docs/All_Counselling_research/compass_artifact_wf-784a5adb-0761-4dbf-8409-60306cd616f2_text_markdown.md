## National counseling: the JoSAA, CSAB and CEPT triangle

### JoSAA — the prestige funnel (~600–650 B.Arch seats nationwide)

JoSAA 2026 will be conducted by **IIT Roorkee** (JEE Advanced organizing institute). The Architecture Aptitude Test (AAT) is on **June 4, 2026**, result **June 7**; JoSAA registration follows immediately, with 6 rounds expected through ~July 20. **Three IITs (Roorkee, Kharagpur, BHU), 10 NITs + IIEST Shibpur, and three SPAs (Delhi/Bhopal/Vijayawada) participate in B.Arch. No IIIT or GFTI offers B.Arch through JoSAA.** A widespread misconception worth correcting on the hub page: BIT Mesra is often listed as a JoSAA institute but admits B.Arch directly via JEE Main Paper 2 score, **not** through JoSAA.

**Critical eligibility rule for IIT B.Arch:** Three stages — JEE Main P1 (qualify for Advanced) → JEE Advanced (clear cutoff: 35% Open, 31.5% OBC/EWS, 17.5% SC/ST/PwD) → AAT (Pass/Fail only; final allotment is on JEE Advanced AIR, not AAT score). Without AAT registration, IIT B.Arch eligibility is forfeited even with a top JEE Advanced rank. This is one of the most asked but poorly explained topics across competitor sites — strong opportunity for a definitive Neram explainer.

**JoSAA 2025 closing ranks (Round 1, OPEN, Gender-Neutral)** for reference:
- SPA Delhi B.Arch: **CRL 118–207** (most competitive B.Arch in India)
- NIT Calicut: HS 208 / OS 232 | NIT Trichy: HS 276 / OS 151
- VNIT Nagpur: HS 398 / OS 617 | IIEST Shibpur: HS 685 / OS 646
- SPA Bhopal: ~400-550 | SPA Vijayawada: ~550-750
- IIT Roorkee B.Arch: AIR 1,500-2,800 (JEE Advanced)
- IIT Kharagpur B.Arch: AIR 2,000-3,500 (JEE Advanced)

**Seat acceptance fee** (per JoSAA 2025 Business Rules): **₹35,000** (GEN/OBC/EWS) or **₹15,000** (SC/ST/PwD), inclusive of ₹5,000 non-refundable JoSAA processing charge. Float/Slide/Freeze options are available except in Round 6. Withdrawal refund deducts ₹7,000 processing on top of the ₹5,000 fee — this exact ₹ math is poorly documented elsewhere and is a definitive-content opportunity.

### CSAB — three distinct verticals (often confused)

CSAB runs three different processes that competitors collapse into one:

1. **CSAB-Special**: Fills NIT+ system vacancies after JoSAA Round 6 (NITs, IIEST, IIITs, SPAs, GFTIs — **NOT IITs**). Three rounds. Fee: ₹40,000 (GEN/OBC/EWS) or ₹19,000 (SC/ST/PwD) as Special Round Enrolment Fee. Plus ₹35,000/₹16,000 IAF-II after allotment.
2. **CSAB-NEUT**: For 8 NE states (Arunachal, Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim, Tripura) + 5 UTs (A&N, DNH&DD, Lakshadweep, Ladakh). ~3,000 seats annually for AICTE-approved programs in institutions outside home state.
3. **CSAB-Supernumerary**: For UTs lacking own NIT (A&N, Lakshadweep, DD, DNH) — supernumerary seats at NIT Calicut, Durgapur, SVNIT Surat.

**Key rule competitors get wrong:** A candidate already allotted in JoSAA who registers for CSAB-Special and gets a new seat — **the JoSAA seat is automatically cancelled and cannot be reverted**. Many students don't realize this risk.

### CEPT University Ahmedabad — independent, NATA-only, premium tier

CEPT does not participate in JoSAA, CSAB, or any central counseling. **NATA mandatory; JEE Main Paper 2 not accepted.** Seat split: **25% via Gujarat ACPC + 75% via CEPT All India** (older 33/67 split is obsolete). Merit: **50% NATA + 50% Class 12 aggregate**. Total program fee approximately **₹22.75 lakh** for 5 years (~₹4.55L/year, rising ~14% YoY). Intake: ~160 seats (varies 120–170 across sources). NIRF 2025 Architecture & Planning rank: **6**. The 2024 NATA closing rank for CEPT B.Arch was around 21,002. CEPT's lack of central-counseling participation, combined with its premium brand, makes it a high-search-volume niche where competitors have particularly thin coverage.

## State counseling deep dive: critical patterns, gotchas, and what to feature

### Tamil Nadu — TNEA B.Arch (your flagship hub)

**This is the single most important hub page for Neram given your TN/Chennai NATA coaching base.** TNEA B.Arch is conducted *separately* from B.Tech TNEA on **barch.tneaonline.org**, on a different timeline (typically a few weeks after B.Tech), with its own random number, rank list and merit formula. Master TNEA 2026 schedule is already published (registration **May 3, 2026**; rank list **June 29**); B.Arch-specific dates TBD post-AICTE approval.

**The 5 things competitors get wrong on Tamil Nadu B.Arch:**
1. The eligibility threshold is **45% PCM aggregate** (not 50% as widely cited) per the 2025 brochure aligned with COA's revised norm.
2. Tamil Nadu does **NOT** separately implement central 10% EWS within its 69% communal reservation matrix — the 69% (BC 26.5% + BCM 3.5% + MBC/DNC 20% + SC 15% + SCA 3% + ST 1%) is protected under the 9th Schedule and operates independently.
3. **7.5% Government School horizontal reservation** (G.O. 167/2021) is unique to TN and provides full fee waiver including hostel — under-explained everywhere.
4. **Crescent, SRM, Sathyabama, Hindustan, Vels, Karpagam are deemed universities and admit B.Arch outside TNEA** (some surrender seats but they are not core TNEA participants). Many guides incorrectly list them as TNEA colleges.
5. Merit formula: **HSC marks scaled to 200 + NATA/JEE P2 (200) = total out of 400**, with 3-tier tiebreak (HSC aggregate → older DOB → larger random number).

**Top TN B.Arch closing marks 2024 (out of 400, OC):** SAP Anna University 339.0 (Govt), 331.2 (Self-Supporting); PSG Coimbatore 322.33; Thiagarajar Madurai 319.33; MEASI 316.67; CARE Trichy 293.8. ~30 colleges allotted in 2024 cycle.

### Maharashtra — MHT-CET CAP B.Arch (Type A–E system)

Maharashtra's distinguishing feature is the **Type A–E candidate categorization** (with rare Type F/G in special categories): Type A = Maharashtra-domiciled with SSC+HSC from Maharashtra; Type B = parent's Maharashtra domicile; Type C = parent posted in Maharashtra by GoI; Type D = Maharashtra State Govt employee parent; Type E = Maharashtra-Karnataka border area resident. Most state-quota seats are 85%; AI/OMS quota 15%. Maharashtra runs **separate inter-se merit lists for NATA candidates and JEE Main Paper 2 candidates** — seat matrices are split. Application fee: ₹800 Open / ₹600 reserved / ₹5,000 NRI/OCI/PIO/FN. The **SEBC (Maratha) reservation status is in legal flux** — flag this in copy.

Sir JJ College of Architecture, BKPS Pune, BNCA Pune, KRVIA Mumbai, Rachana Sansad, Pillai New Panvel, Rizvi Mumbai, Allana Pune, Anjuman-I-Islam are the major participants. Linguistic minority colleges (Pillai for Malayali, Rizvi/Allana for Muslim) carry up to 51% community quota. **Sir JJ's CAP Round-1 closing for OPEN** has been NATA ~138-145 (HS) / 150+ (OMS) and JEE P2 ~94-96 percentile in recent years.

### Andhra Pradesh and Telangana — separate post-bifurcation, common confusions

Both states use Article 371D Presidential Order with **85% Local + 15% Unreserved**. AP has two local areas (AU – 8 coastal districts; SVU – Rayalaseema 5 districts). Telangana is a single OU local area. **Critical reservation distinction**: Telangana ST is **10%** (raised from 6% in October 2022); AP ST stayed at **6%**. Both have BC-A 7%, BC-B 10%, BC-C 1%, BC-D 7%, BC-E 4% (subject to legal review).

**Naming change to feature prominently**: TS EAMCET → **TG EAPCET** (from 2024); TSCHE → **TGCHE**. The B.Arch portal moved to **barchadm.tgche.ac.in**. Many students still search "TS EAMCET B.Arch" — your hub page should target both terms and explain the rebrand.

**Critical clarification (worth a dedicated FAQ):** SPA Vijayawada is NOT in APSCHE counseling (it's a CFTI via JoSAA). JNAFAU Hyderabad School of Planning & Architecture is in TGCHE only (not AP). NIT Warangal/Calicut B.Arch routes through JoSAA. Eligibility for both states: **NATA or JEE Main P2**, not the EAPCET/EAMCET state exam. JNAFAU SPA Hyderabad's 2025 OC closing was around State Architecture Rank 21 (NATA ~105 normalized).

### Karnataka and Kerala — both NATA-only states, with twists

**Karnataka KEA explicitly does NOT conduct any CET for B.Arch.** Per KEA notification: admission via NATA or JEE Main Paper 2 only. Best-of-both is used when both are submitted. The **Article 371(J) Kalyana Karnataka reservation** (Bidar, Kalaburagi, Yadgir, Raichur, Koppal, Ballari, Vijayanagar) provides 70% local seats in colleges within the region and 8% statewide outside — this is a unique nuance worth a focused explainer. **COMEDK does NOT conduct an entrance for B.Arch** either; they only run a counseling using NATA/JEE P2 scores. This dual KEA + COMEDK + Management quota structure is poorly explained on every competitor site.

**Kerala KEAM exam itself excludes B.Arch** — only NATA score is used for the architecture rank list (per CEE Notification 30-06-2025). JEE Main Paper 2 acceptance for Kerala B.Arch is not officially mentioned in 2025 — verify in 2026 prospectus. Kerala uses a **3-category nativity system** (Keralite / NK-I / NK-II) with reservation benefits restricted to Keralite. Communal reservation (SEBC 30%) is detailed: Ezhava 9%, Muslim 8%, BH 3%, LA 3%, DV 2%, VK 2%, KN 1%, BX 1%, KU 1%. **NIT Calicut B.Arch is NOT through KEAM** — JoSAA only — a top reader confusion.

CET Trivandrum B.Arch closing General rank in 2025 was 61 (overall closing 674); in 2024 it was 36 (overall 2,206). This is among the most competitive state B.Arch admits in India.

### Delhi and Chandigarh — small but high-prestige hubs

**JAC Delhi B.Arch is essentially IGDTUW only (women-only)** in 2025. NSUT B.Arch via JAC is discontinued/limited. SPA Delhi is via JoSAA, not JAC. Registration fee is **flat ₹1,500 for all categories** (the widely reported "₹500 SC/ST" is incorrect for JAC Delhi 2025). Region split: **85% Delhi / 15% Outside Delhi**. IGDTUW B.Arch Round-1 General closing for Delhi region in 2025 was CRL ~1,954 (JEE Main P2A).

**JAC Chandigarh B.Arch is exclusively Chandigarh College of Architecture (CCA), Sector 12** — 40 seats, NIRF Architecture rank 30. PEC Chandigarh does NOT offer B.Arch. CCA is among the most affordable top B.Arch institutes in India: **₹30,000/year tuition, ₹1.5 lakh total for 5 years**. 2025 Round-1 General closing rank (Outside Chandigarh): CRL 164. Quota: 32 Chandigarh + 6 Outside + 1 Kashmiri Pandit + 1 Kargil Martyr ward. Registration fee: ₹2,800 General/EWS, ₹1,400 SC/ST/PwD; seat acceptance ₹40,000.

### The "where B.Arch state counseling barely exists" reality

This is a content-truthfulness opportunity. The states below have very limited B.Arch through their own counseling, and most aspirants better served by JoSAA/CSAB:

**Bihar (BCECEB UGEAC)**: Only Gaya College of Engineering offers B.Arch through state counseling; NIT Patna routes via JoSAA. Counseling is **offline** (distinct from B.Tech UGEAC online).
**Jharkhand (JCECEB)**: No dedicated B.Arch counseling listed. **BIT Mesra (autonomous, ~40 B.Arch seats)** admits directly via JEE Main P2; Awadh College Jamshedpur and JUT Ranchi via NATA. NIT Jamshedpur and BIT Sindri do NOT offer B.Arch.
**Chhattisgarh (DTE/CG-Vyapam)**: CG-PET has no B.Arch component. Most state pool is Amity Raipur and small private colleges. NIT Raipur B.Arch via JoSAA. IIT Bhilai does NOT offer B.Arch.
**Goa (DTE Goa)**: Single CoA-recognized college — Goa College of Architecture (GCA), Panaji, with 40 + 4 supernumerary seats; total course fee ₹3.04 lakh.
**Himachal (HPTU)**: ~4 B.Arch colleges; NIT Hamirpur and IIT Mandi do NOT offer B.Arch. HPCET 2026: May 9-10.
**J&K (JKBOPEE)**: Only SMVDU Katra is centrally-funded B.Arch in J&K. NIT Srinagar and IIT Jammu do NOT offer B.Arch. JKCET 2026: April 19.
**Northeast (DTE Assam + state DTEs)**: Outside Assam, most NE states have zero or one B.Arch institute. NIT Silchar B.Arch via JoSAA only; CSAB-NEUT is the cross-state route for AICTE seats.
**Uttarakhand (VMSB-UTU)**: UKSEE was discontinued in 2023; admissions now NATA/JEE P2 + counseling. IIT Roorkee B.Arch is JoSAA via JEE Advanced + AAT, not UTU.

These thin-pool states are *opportunities for honest, "what to actually do" content* that competitors avoid because they want to monetize lead generation across all states.

### Other state-level counselings — quick reference

**West Bengal (WBJEEB)**: WBJEE has no separate Architecture paper; B.Arch admissions use JEE Main P2 or NATA via WBJEEB-coordinated counseling. Only 2 main rounds + Mop-up in 2025 (revised from 3). Jadavpur University B.Arch (40 seats, NIRF 16) is top — and at ₹26,050 total for 5 years, the cheapest premier B.Arch in India. IIEST Shibpur does NOT offer B.Arch (only B.Tech).

**Odisha (OJEEC)**: OJEE itself doesn't test B.Arch; admissions via NATA/JEE P2. CET Bhubaneswar (OUTR, 20 seats) is top govt option (~₹2.84L total). NIT Rourkela B.Arch via JoSAA. **Major reservation flux for 2026**: A December 2025 Odisha Policy Planning Body recommendation proposes raising SC to 16.25%, ST to 22.5%, and introducing 11.25% SEBC — implementation pending notification.

**Gujarat (ACPC)**: 75% home-state (Gujarat + DD + DNH school candidates) + 25% all-India. **CEPT participation in ACPC is now uncertain** — CEPT mostly admits independently. Reservation: SC 7%, ST 15%, SEBC 27%, EWS 10%.

**UP (UPTAC/AKTU)**: Uses NATA priority + JEE Main P2 secondary. UPCET (NTA) does NOT cover B.Arch. Domicile rule: parent's UP domicile OR qualifying exam from UP institution. **No tuition fee waiver for B.Arch under UPTAC** (only B.Tech/B.Pharm/MCA/MBA). 4 main rounds + sliding + special + spot.

**Rajasthan (REAP)**: Uses NATA/JEE P2; MNIT Jaipur via JoSAA. Application fee ₹590. Women's horizontal reservation reported as 25% or 30% depending on year.

**MP (DTE-MP)**: 90% MP domicile quota. **OBC reservation in active Supreme Court litigation** — operating under interim 87:13 formula (14% OBC effective; the 27% notified figure pending verdict in *Shivam Gautam v. State of MP*, 2025).

**Haryana (HSTES)**: PPP ID (Parivar Pehchan Patra) mandatory for Haryana candidates. NATA mandatory for B.Arch (50:50 NATA + 12th merit). Complex sub-quotas: HOGC, RoHC, HGST, plus 25% horizontal female reservation. NIT Kurukshetra B.Arch via JoSAA.

**Punjab (IKGPTU)**: Counseling fee ₹2,000 per course. 85% Punjab + 15% other state. SC reservation 25% (sub-classification under post-2024 *Davinder Singh* Constitution Bench framework). PEC, CCA, GNDU, LPU, Chandigarh University, Chitkara all admit outside IKGPTU centralized counseling.

## Cross-cutting facts every hub page should standardize

Five universal eligibility/process facts that should be presented identically across all hub pages, with the inevitable state nuances flagged inline:

**Council of Architecture eligibility (2024-25 norm, applied 2026)**: 10+2 with Physics + Mathematics compulsory (third subject from Chemistry/Biology/CS/IT/IP/Engg Graphics/Business Studies/Vocational), **45% aggregate** (some state counselings still apply 50%); OR 10+3 Diploma with Mathematics, 45-50% aggregate. **Single-board issuance** required.

**Entrance accepted**: NATA (Council of Architecture, multi-phase) and/or JEE Main Paper 2A (NTA, two sessions). State acceptance varies — KEAM accepts NATA only (not JEE P2 per 2025 notification); Maharashtra accepts both with separate merit lists; Karnataka/Tamil Nadu/Gujarat use best-of-both.

**Common merit formula**: 50% qualifying examination (PCM aggregate or 12th overall) + 50% NATA/JEE P2 score. Different states normalize differently — TN scales to 200+200=400; Kerala uses 50:50 weights; AP/TG compute SAR (State Architecture Rank).

**Validity of NATA score**: Two academic years (NATA 2024 valid for 2025-26 admissions; some states accept best across multiple attempts). Confirm 2026 brochure for any change.

**B.Arch is 5 years (10 semesters)** mandated by COA. Lateral entry into 2nd year is rare; transfer policies vary.

## Competitive landscape — what the eight major sites do, and where they fail

CollegeDunia leads on **JoSAA/CSAB depth and college predictor integration** but treats B.Arch as a JEE sub-topic with no unified hub. Shiksha leads on **state nuance for nativity/domicile rules** and college-level cutoff filters, but B.Arch is buried under `/b-tech/` URLs. Careers360 has **the most state-by-state B.Arch admission articles** (TNEA, MHT-CET, AP, TS, UPTAC, MP, Himachal, JAC Delhi/Chandigarh, COMEDK, OJEE, Karnataka, Kerala, Rajasthan) — this is the closest competitor in structure but content is templated and inconsistent in quality. AglaSem is **a PDF archive** with stale text content and active comment sections (long-tail traffic). Embibe is **severely outdated** — many pages still reference 2021-2023 cycles in 2026; their conceptual explainers (Freeze/Float/Slide, JoSAA vs CSAB) rank but content is stale, an easy supersession opportunity. CollegeDekho runs a **news + evergreen hybrid** with state pages but suffers from regional-language leakage and templated copy. CollegeSearch/GetMyUni are **lead-gen heavy with shallow content**. Toprankers is the closest direct coaching competitor with a NATA-counseling page.

**The structural pattern winning organic traffic:** A mix of (a) state-by-state admission articles with status flags in H1 ("(Round 2 Out)", "(Spot Round Started)"), (b) FAQ schema with 15-25 Q&As, (c) round-wise news velocity backlinking to evergreen hubs, (d) college-level cutoff sub-pages, (e) "Last Updated" timestamps with author bylines for E-E-A-T, and (f) a college predictor tool funneling lead capture.

**Universal weaknesses across all eight competitors that Neram can exploit:**
- **No B.Arch-first information architecture** anywhere — every site treats it as B.Tech-adjacent
- **NATA-specific counseling** is consistently summarized as "no centralized counseling exists" with no follow-through into state-by-state NATA acceptance maps, validity rules, or NATA-only college lists
- **Decision-tree content is missing** ("Should I freeze or float at rank X?", "NATA-only or JEE P2 — which exam should I prioritize?", "When can I participate in two state counselings simultaneously?")
- **Post-allotment timeline** (seat acceptance → reporting → joining → Day 1) is uncovered everywhere
- **Refund/withdrawal mechanics** with exact ₹ deductions per round per state are sparsely documented
- **DASA (foreign/OCI/NRI) B.Arch counseling** is barely covered
- **Document discrepancy resolution** (category certificate rejection, name mismatch, post-2014 OBC certificates) gets brief mentions only
- **Architecture-specific reservation policies** (B.Arch women's seats, COA approval status by college) are largely absent
- **Cross-state same-day clashes** (TNEA B.Arch reporting vs JoSAA seat acceptance deadline) get no coverage despite being a top student pain point

## Critical caveats for the editorial team before publishing

Three categories of risk to manage in your editorial QA:

**Numbers and percentages with conflicting sources need verification before publishing each page.** Karnataka SC/ST is reported as 15%/3% in some KEA brochures and 17%/7% per a 2023 GO; Kerala State Merit varies between 50% and 60% across notifications; Punjab BC reservation has evolved from 5% to 10% to 12%; Rajasthan women's horizontal is reported as 25% or 30%; MP OBC is in active Supreme Court litigation with operative 14% under 87:13 formula; Odisha SC/ST/SEBC percentages are pending a December 2025 revision proposal; JAC Delhi registration fee is flat ₹1,500 (not ₹1,500/₹500 split as stated in the original task brief); Bihar reverted to 50%+10% reservation framework after Patna HC's June 2024 ruling. **Always cross-verify against the official 2026 brochure when released, not aggregator sites.**

**Some Tier-1 brief assumptions need correction in the published copy.** The TNEA 2025 brochure states 45% PCM (not 50%); JAC Delhi B.Arch is essentially IGDTUW only (NSUT discontinued); CCA Chandigarh is the sole JAC Chandigarh B.Arch institute (PEC has no B.Arch); SPA Vijayawada is JoSAA not APSCHE; JNAFAU Hyderabad is in TGCHE (Telangana) not AP; NIT Calicut is JoSAA not KEAM; NIT Patna is JoSAA not BCECEB; NIT Rourkela is JoSAA not OJEE; MNIT Jaipur is JoSAA not REAP; NIT Hamirpur, IIT Mandi, NIT Srinagar, IIT Jammu, IIT Bhilai, NIT Jamshedpur, BIT Sindri all do NOT offer B.Arch; IIEST Shibpur does NOT offer B.Arch.

**Cycle freshness must be flagged on every page.** Until each authority releases its 2026 brochure, the page should display "Based on 2025 cycle — 2026 dates expected by [month]" labels next to schedule blocks. This honesty is itself a differentiator: every competitor mixes 2024/2025/2026 dates in the same article without flagging.
